In the beginning only the biological brain was considered to be an intelligent system. Now recent advances in machine learning have resulted in non-biological devices which are capable of exhibiting 'intelligent' behavior at least in a restricted sense. Indeed, artificial general intelligence (AGI) still remains a distance dream.

Now to understand general intelligence we need to understand both the biological brain and the machine learning algorithms which redner an agent some intelligence.

In this meeting we bring together experts from Mathematics, Artificial Intelligence and Neuroscience to discuss the nature of intelligence. 1
